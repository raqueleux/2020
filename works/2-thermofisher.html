We help our customers accelerate life sciences research, solve complex analytical challenges, improve patient diagnostics and increase laboratory productivity.


Minimum Viable Product

Customers have the ability to use the thermofisher.com website to enter and upload their orders.  Once they navigate to the custom orders page, they can select any product. The standard section has 3 links: Single, Bulk Entry, or Next-day.  Upon successful configuration and validation of their product, the customer will add to cart and proceed through the check-out process to submit their order.


Registration Development

Identity design followed a twofold strategy:

  1. Change the portal for the standard section, streamlining the experience by creating a multistep UI page, easy steps, and cleaner error messages.
  2. Full validation will occur after the user has entered their sequence, selected scale, modifications, and clicks the validate button.








A not-so-great-product-launch

Following usability results, we re-launched the product as a single step form. New brand colors improved perceived authenticity of the product. User feedback emphasised that the form were not very easy to read and prompted an input font increase which extended to accessibility standards. This directly impacted purchasing potential and marketing efforts. The solution, placed us in an iteration cycle that lasted over a month. Changes in input height, altering type size, adding and reducing features: each of this was A/B tested with significant statistical and qualitative outcome.

Not Just a Set of Steps
Addressing problems where multiple spheres collide, at the intersection of business and society, logic and emotion, rational and creative, human needs and economic demands and between systems and individuals. UX design should not only to be understood as a process or method for solving a rigid set of problems. It is the mindset that can be applied in almost any scenario where innovation or applying user centric concepts is required. It can also be combined with other methodologies, business strategies, social innovation models, and management practices.


It's About Human-Centered Innovation

Design Thinking works best where we need to make human sense of things, approaching challenges in ways that best suit human needs regardless of the scale or authority of the challenge. A conformist, controlled, technical or linear approach is no longer able to grapple with the newly complex and sensitive needs of modern society. It starts with an intention, a desire, a need or yearning towards a better situation or state. We have no way of knowing whether this is a mere dream or a practical and viable path to take.
